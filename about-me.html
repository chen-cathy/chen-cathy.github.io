About me

Cathy Chen

Context & trajectory

Background

I am currently based in Prague.

My background includes working across organizational contexts in Taiwan, China, and the Czech Republic—particularly in areas where systems, people, and institutional requirements intersect.

I hold a master's degree in Information and Knowledge Management from Loughborough University.

My professional experience spans roles within a French multinational retail group, where I was involved in the design and operation of corporate training systems, knowledge management infrastructure, and organizational processes during mergers and acquisitions.

I later worked in roles positioned at the interface between organizational governance and labor regulation within Taiwanese and Chinese listed companies, engaging with organizational restructuring, cross-border coordination, and labor relations across differing regulatory environments.

In recent years in the Czech Republic, my professional background included involvement in international trade related to the import of products from Taiwan, as well as work in translation, providing practical exposure to cross-regulatory and cross-linguistic contexts.

I am currently undertaking graduate studies in Czech at the Prague University of Economics and Business, Faculty of Management Master's studies of Czech program.

How I read organizations

Approach

My background allows me to translate between different managerial logics without reducing them to stereotypes.

I read formal rules alongside informal practices, and I pay attention to how policies are absorbed, adapted, or quietly resisted inside organizations.

This involves looking at how people make sense of rules, carry them out in daily operations, and work around them when institutional constraints collide.

This perspective has proven especially relevant in contexts where Asian business approaches meet European institutional expectations.

I am someone who thinks across systems, who is comfortable moving between practice and theory, and who prefers to make reasoning explicit rather than authoritative.

This dialogue is not an attempt by AI to define who I am. Instead, it shows how recurring questions can reveal patterns of concern—sometimes noticed by the AI before they become explicit to the person asking them.

People do not usually articulate their values through everyday actions, but in dialogue with AI, those values often emerge indirectly, through the questions they return to again and again.
