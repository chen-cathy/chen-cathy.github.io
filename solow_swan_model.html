Solow-Swan Model: Capital Accumulation & Convergence

January 2026 |

Executive Summary

This report consolidates the analysis of the Solow-Swan growth model applied to selected economies (Singapore, Taiwan, Czech Republic, South Korea). Part I examines the capital accumulation phase and GDP growth trends compared to the theoretical exponential model. Part II analyzes the convergence hypothesis, testing both \(\beta\)-convergence (catch-up effect) and \(\sigma\)-convergence (reduction in dispersion) over the period 2000–2024.

Část I: Solowův-Swanův model akumulace kapitálu

The first part of our analysis focuses on the long-term growth trajectory. Using the Solow-Swan framework, we model the GDP per capita growth as an exponential function \( y(t) = A e^{gt} \), where \(g\) represents the balanced growth rate driven by technological progress and capital accumulation.

Část II: Konvergence (resp. divergence/stagnace) ekonomik

The second part investigates whether these economies are converging. \(\beta\)-convergence implies that poorer economies grow faster than richer ones (negative correlation between initial level and growth rate). \(\sigma\)-convergence refers to the reduction of income dispersion (standard deviation) over time.
